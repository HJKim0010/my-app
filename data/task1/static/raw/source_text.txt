Task 1 - The Day Everything Went Wrong

Jack had been preparing for his final team project presentation for weeks. His team was depending on him to lead the talk. He practiced every night, checking each slide carefully, and set two alarms before sleeping.

That morning, Jack opened his eyes and saw bright sunlight flooding his room. He was shocked. He jumped out of bed and looked at the clock. He was already very late. His alarms had failed.

In a rush, he grabbed his clothes, laptop, and bag. He raced out the door without eating breakfast. At the subway station, he patted his pockets and realized he had forgotten his wallet and student ID. He sighed, but had no time to return.

The train was crowded and slow. Jack squeezed onto the train, holding his bag tightly. As he tried to check his notes on his phone, it slipped from his sweaty hand and fell under a seat. He bent down to pick it up. Suddenly, the train moved quickly, and Jack lost his balance. A woman standing near him caught his arm and helped him stand.

"You dropped this," she said, handing him a folded paper. Jack opened it. The handwriting was neat but unfamiliar. The message said: "Stay on the train. Get off at the last station."

Jack looked up, but the woman had disappeared into the crowd. He sat back down, feeling tense. Was this a joke? A real warning? The train passed one station after another. Jack was thinking about his team. Should he trust the strange message? Or should he get off early to reach the classroom on time? Every minute mattered. His final grade and his graduation could depend on what he decided.
